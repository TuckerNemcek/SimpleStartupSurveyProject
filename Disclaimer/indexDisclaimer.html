The Simple Financial Health Check assumes Accrual Accounting basis (rather than Cash Accounting). Accrual Accounting is the preferred accounting basis as it provides a more precise picture of the health of your business at any point in time even if cash has not changed hands.

Cash Accounting affects things like COGS and Inventory. For example, imagine you purchased $25,000 worth of Raw Materials in January for a $50,000 order in February. Under Cash Accounting, you would have a $25,000 loss in January and a $50,000 Sale in February with no COGS. This reality distorts ratio analysis which is why the Financial Health Check assumes Accrual Accounting.

Please be advised Accrual Accounting is required by the IRS if you carry inventory (as opposed to a service business with no inventory), if you are structured as a C Corporation, or of your sales exceed $5 Million. The final disclaimer is the Simple Financial Health Check assumes reporting accuracy.
